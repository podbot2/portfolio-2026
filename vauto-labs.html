01
What is vAuto Labs?

An experimental platform for dealers who try new features first.

vAuto Labs is a new feature platform within ProfitTime GPS that gives beta dealers early access to vAuto's newest tools before they launch to the broader ~220,000-user base. Dealers can enable features directly, join waitlists for upcoming releases, and submit thumbs-up/down feedback from within Labs.

Alpha launched March 3, 2026 · 6 hand-selected dealers · Beta and GA timelines TBD pending alpha research findings.

Jennifer Flores — Designer Monica Herrera — Product Rob Monk — Pendo Admin Patrick Dennis — PM
02
The problem

Introducing a new platform across two pages, without losing the user between them.

Pendo guides cannot span multiple pages — each guide is scoped to a single URL. Labs lives on ExperimentPlatform.aspx. The announcement that dealers need to see first fires on Provision.aspx, the ProfitTime dashboard they land on at login.

The handoff between Guide 1 (announcement) and Guide 2 (onboarding) had to feel seamless — not like two disconnected pop-ups. The user clicks 'Explore Labs →' in Guide 1, lands on the Labs page, and Guide 2 fires immediately. If the timing or framing is off, dealers bounce before Labs has a chance to explain itself.

Compounding this: dealer research at vAuto shows that users are not accustomed to self-service. They default to calling for help. Labs only works at scale if dealers can discover, understand, and activate features on their own.

03
The solution

Two guides. One experience.

Guide 1 — Announcement
The entry point.
Fires on: ProfitTime dashboard (Provision.aspx)
Trigger: First visit only
Format: Full-screen lightbox overlay

vAuto Labs

See and try the new tools we are working on.

Explore Labs →

Guide 2 — Labs Onboarding (3 steps)
The activation path.
Fires on: Labs page (ExperimentPlatform.aspx)
Trigger: Automatically on arrival from Guide 1
1
See what's being tested
These are features available to your dealership. Turn one on to add it to your workflow, or ask your admin to enable it. Some features enable directly. Others require a browser extension from the app store.
2
Get information on any feature
Use the interactive tutorial to walk through a feature step by step. Check the FAQ for quick answers to common questions.
Anchor: Tutorial and FAQ links on feature cards
3
Show interest in upcoming features
Join the waitlist to request access. If a spot opens, your admin can turn the feature on for your dealership.
CTA: Done
04
Design rationale

Every decision had a reason.

01
Two-guide structure
Pendo cannot span pages. Guide 1 fires on the ProfitTime dashboard; Guide 2 fires on Labs. The handoff is designed to feel seamless — Guide 1 completes, the CTA navigates to Labs, and Guide 2 fires immediately on arrival.
02
No nav beacon callout
The "Explore Labs" CTA navigates directly to Labs. Adding an intermediate beacon step would break momentum and add friction between discovery and arrival.
03
Feedback step removed
Thumbs up/down feedback is already visible on feature cards in Labs. Adding a guide step to call it out would serve a product goal, not a user goal. Enterprise transfers guide data showed only 3 responses — a partner activation problem, not a guide design one.
04
Tutorial/FAQ as Step 2, not last
Dealer research showed dealers were missing Tutorial and FAQ links because they didn't know they existed. Placing it as Step 2 — not appended as padding at the end — surfaces it while attention is highest.
05
Admin vs. non-admin copy
Both audiences receive the same guide. Admins read "turn one on" as their action. Non-admins read "ask your admin" as theirs. No separate guide versions needed.
06
~180-character limit for feature descriptions
Established a working character limit for feature card descriptions to keep content scannable and consistent across all features that appear in Labs.
05
Measuring success

Listening before declaring it works.

Moderated interviews scheduled 1–2 weeks post-alpha with the 6-dealer cohort. Goal: qualitative signal to inform changes before beta or GA — not to validate that launch succeeded. Findings feed directly into beta synthesis with no separate alpha readout.

1
Dealer understanding
Can dealers explain what Labs is and their role, without prompting?
2
Feature adoption intent
Did dealers attempt to enable a feature? What drove or blocked that?
3
Usability gaps
Were there moments of confusion, friction, or missing context?
4
Feedback loop quality
Did dealers notice and use the thumbs up/down? Do they understand how their input is used?

Pendo data to pull before calls:

Guide 1 views, Guide 1→2 handoff completion, Guide 2 step-level drop-off, time-on-step, Labs page return visits, feature toggle clicks, Tutorial/FAQ click rate.

06
GA Readiness

What does GA actually require?

Five dimensions define beta readiness. The overarching question: "Does vAuto Labs create visible dealer value, accelerate learning and iterative development, without introducing risk — at scale?"

01
Dealer value & engagement is proven
Dealers understand Labs, use it intentionally, and can self-discover, self-service, and self-support.
02
Labs works as a scalable platform
Functions reliably with minimal manual intervention. Dealers can access, enable/disable, and navigate without hand-holding.
03
Feedback loops are consistent and actionable
At least one repeatable feedback mechanism dealers actually use. Feedback is understandable and tied to specific experiments.
04
Product teams can validate ideas faster
Labs used to validate problem-solution fit, identify usability gaps early, and kill or pivot ideas before heavy investment.
05
Internal readiness & confidence
Product, Engineering, Support, and PMs understand what Labs is and is not, and know how to respond when something goes wrong.
