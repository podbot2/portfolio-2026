vAuto Labs

An experimental feature platform within ProfitTime GPS that lets beta
dealers discover, enable, and give feedback on new features before GA release.

vAuto / Cox Automotive

Alpha: March 2026

Q1, 2026

Role: Lead UX Designer

PROJECT OVERVIEW

vAuto Labs is a new experimental feature platform within ProfitTime GPS that allows beta dealers to try features before they launch to the broader vAuto user base (~220,000 users).

Dealers can enable features, join waitlists for upcoming features, and submit feedback. The platform launched in alpha on March 3, 2026, starting with 6 hand-selected dealers — visible to all users at those dealerships with no permission gating.

My role was to design the full onboarding and discovery experience — including the Pendo guide sequence that introduces dealers to Labs, a two-guide system spanning the ProfitTime dashboard and the Labs page.

01/

Bridging two pages with one seamless handoff.

guide design

Because Pendo guides cannot span across pages, I designed a two-guide system that creates a seamless handoff from the ProfitTime dashboard to the Labs page. The transition is invisible to the user — it feels like a single continuous experience.

Guide 1 — Announcement

Fires on the ProfitTime dashboard (Provision.aspx) as a full-screen lightbox overlay on a dealer's first visit only. No element anchor — it appears over the page to command attention.

Guide copy

Title: vAuto Labs
Body: See and try the new tools we are working on.
CTA: Explore Labs → (navigates directly to Labs page)

Guide 2 — Labs Onboarding (3 steps)

Fires automatically on the Labs page immediately following Guide 1 completion. Three steps, each with a specific anchor and a distinct user goal.

Step 1 — See what's being tested

These are features available to your dealership. Turn one on to add it to your workflow, or ask your admin to enable it. Some features enable directly. Others require a browser extension from the app store.

Step 2 — Get information on any feature

Use the interactive tutorial to walk through a feature step by step. Check the FAQ for quick answers to common questions.
Anchor: Tutorial and FAQ links on feature cards

Step 3 — Show interest in upcoming features

Join the waitlist to request access. If a spot opens, your admin can turn the feature on for your dealership.
CTA: Done

02/

Every decision had a reason.

key UX decisions

Design Rationale

Two-guide structure

/01

Required because Pendo cannot span pages. The handoff between Guide 1 and Guide 2 is engineered to feel seamless — Guide 1 completes, the dealer navigates to Labs, and Guide 2 fires immediately on arrival.

No nav beacon callout

/02

The "Explore Labs" CTA in Guide 1 navigates directly to Labs without a separate beacon step. Adding an intermediate beacon would break momentum and add unnecessary friction to the discovery flow.

Feedback step removed

/03

Thumbs up/down is visible on feature cards in Labs. Adding a guide step to call it out would serve a product goal, not a user goal. The enterprise transfers guide showed only 3 responses — a partner activation problem, not a guide design one.

Tutorial/FAQ as Step 2

/04

Dealer research showed dealers were missing the Tutorial and FAQ links because they didn't know they existed. Placing it as Step 2 — not appended last — prevents it from feeling like filler padding and surfaces it where attention is highest.

Admin vs. non-admin copy

/05

Copy is written to serve both audiences simultaneously. Admins read "turn one on" as their action. Non-admins read "ask your admin" as theirs. No separate guide versions needed — the framing resolves the split naturally.

~180 character limit for feature descriptions

/06

Established a working character limit of approximately 180 characters for feature card descriptions. This ensures content stays scannable and consistent across all features that appear in Labs.

03/

Listening before declaring success.

alpha research plan

Moderated interviews with the 6-dealer alpha cohort, scheduled 1–2 weeks post-release. The goal is qualitative signal to inform changes before beta or GA — not to validate that launch succeeded. Findings feed directly into beta synthesis with no separate readout for the alpha cohort.

Research goals (priority order)

01 — Dealer understanding

Can dealers explain what Labs is and their role in it, without prompting? Comprehension gaps here signal copy or framing failures in the guides.

02 — Feature adoption intent

Did dealers attempt to enable a feature? What drove or blocked that action? This cross-references Pendo toggle data against self-report.

03 — Usability gaps

Were there moments of confusion, friction, or missing context? Patterns appearing across 2+ dealers indicate a systematic issue worth addressing before beta.

04 — Feedback loop quality

Did dealers notice and use the thumbs up/down? Do they understand how their input is used? Low awareness may point to UI visibility, not engagement intent.

Pendo data to pull before interviews

Guide performance

Guide 1 views — did all alpha dealers see the announcement modal? Guide 1 → Guide 2 handoff — did completion trigger Guide 2? Guide 2 step-level drop-off and time-on-step to identify where attention was lost.

Post-guide behavior

Labs page return visits, feature toggle clicks, Tutorial/FAQ clicks, waitlist interactions, and thumbs up/down usage (near-zero expected at alpha). Cross-reference against Pendo data before attributing gaps to usability or intent.

04/

What does GA readiness actually mean?

beta success criteria

Five dimensions define GA readiness for vAuto Labs. The central question: "Does vAuto Labs create visible dealer value, accelerate learning and iterative development, without introducing risk — at scale?"

GA Readiness Dimensions

Dealer value & engagement is proven

/01

Dealers understand Labs, use it intentionally, and see value. They can self-discover, self-service, and self-support. GA signal: dealers view Labs as a safe, intentional place to try innovation — not a confusing or risky beta dump.

Labs works as a scalable platform

/02

Platform functions reliably with minimal manual intervention. Dealers can access, enable/disable, and navigate without hand-holding. GA signal: minimal critical incidents; known limitations documented; admin tooling sufficient for enrollment.

Feedback loops are consistent and actionable

/03

At least one repeatable feedback mechanism dealers actually use. Feedback is understandable, tied to specific experiments, and actionable. GA signal: feedback is no longer ad hoc or anecdotal — it's built into the Labs process.

Product teams can validate ideas faster

/04

Labs used to validate problem-solution fit, identify usability gaps early, and kill or pivot ideas before heavy investment. GA signal: teams trust Labs as a decision-making tool, not just a launch checkbox.

Internal readiness & confidence exists across teams

/05

Product, Engineering, Support, and PMs understand what Labs is and is not, know how to explain it to dealers, and know how to respond when something goes wrong. GA signal: vAuto can stand behind Labs as a standard experiment testing process, not a special exception.
